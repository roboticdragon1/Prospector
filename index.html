Closed to rock collecting
Lavender fill = no collecting

The lavender shading marks federal land where collecting rocks, minerals, or fossils is prohibited or the ground is withdrawn from mineral entry: National Parks, National Monuments, National Wildlife Refuges, and designated Wilderness / Wilderness Study Areas (plus all Park Service & Fish-&-Wildlife land). Source: USGS PAD-US.

No lavender ≠ open to collect. This shows the big federal closures only. Unshaded ground can still be off-limits — state or local rules, a special withdrawal, an active claim, or private land. The safe read is "lavender = definitely closed," never "unshaded = allowed." Confirm local rules before you dig.

Claims load for the area on screen when you're zoomed in past ~town level. Pan, then tap ⟳ Claims to refresh. Source: BLM Mineral & Land Records System (MLRS) — the same official data The Diggings uses. Squares cover the claim's PLSS area and may be larger than the actual claim. A dashed square marks a claim whose BLM-mapped boundary is low-confidence (approximate edges) — the claim itself is just as real.

Two ways a real claim can look missing. The squares show every claim BLM still lists as open — active and still-in-process ("Filed"). But a claim that's been closed (e.g. the "Rainbow Lode" USGS record) won't show as a square — and the ground may still be re-claimed under a different owner's name. And a small number of BLM cases have no mapped boundary at all, so they appear in no polygon layer. When it matters, verify the exact spot 👇
